Lithium in Brines, Clays, Salts, and Spodumene

Lithium is a lightweight metal essential for rechargeable batteries used in electric vehicles, portable electronics, and energy storage systems.

  • Brines: Underground saline waters with high lithium concentrations.
  • Clays: Mineral-rich clays containing lithium, such as hectorite.
  • Salts: Evaporated deposits where lithium has precipitated.
  • Spodumene: A lithium-rich mineral found in pegmatite rocks.

Spodumene Concentrate (5.5% Li₂O)

Spodumene concentrate is derived from spodumene ore and contains approximately 5.5% lithium oxide (Li₂O). It serves as a primary source for lithium extraction.

Spodumene Ore (Crushed to Size)

Spodumene ore is a lithium-bearing mineral that, when crushed to specific sizes, is prepared for processing to extract lithium compounds.

Gallium

Gallium is a soft, silvery metal used in electronics, semiconductors, and optoelectronic devices like LEDs and solar panels.

Scandium

Scandium is a silvery-white metal that enhances the strength and durability of aluminum alloys, used in aerospace, sports equipment, and 3D printing.
